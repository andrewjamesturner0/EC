# Goal
 - Analyse the transcripts in `transcripts/` and create a detailed summaries of key ideas of the most actionable training advice. (Summaries of key ideas should then be grouped thematically.)

# Sub-goals
- Structure: Themes (short organising categories) > Summaries (multiple, delarative subheadings with 200-300 words per section, with bullet point recommendations)
- Summaries should be thematically grouped by high-level general training topics - e.g. Threshold, VO2 max, Endurance, Recovery, Nutrician
- Summaries should be multi-layered. Starting with declarative actionable headlines, then adding detail to the explanation, then appropriate qualifications or caveats.
- Summaries should show longitudinal awareness. Views may evolve accross this corpus and the summaries should reflect this.
- Summaries are not procuded chronologically or by airtime. Instead, they identifies patterns of meaning across the corpus, grouping related ideas into coherent, conceptually precise themes and summaries of key ideas within those themes. It prioritizes conceptual significance and implications rather than airtime.
- Summaries should be  professional, applied, and advisory tone suitable for researchers, practitioners, or policymakers. 
- The summaries addtionally convert the most important ideas into concise, task-oriented recommendations that can be implemented immediately.
- The corpus contains content that is scientifically detailed (e.g. about physiological and biological mechanisms) - the _scientific_ detail is not what is most important for the summaries.
- The corpus is too large to load into context in one go.


# Approach 
## Phase 1
 - Design an analysis approach to achieve these goals. The corpus is large, so some chunking will be necessary.
 - Determine whether it is possible to measure the content similarity of transcripts, to intelligently chunk them into likely related subsets.
## Phase 2
- Conduct the analysis.
