Core Message (partial — based on usable portion only)

Kolie Moore and Kyle Helson set out to debunk myths about fat and carb burning as they relate to fueling and metabolism. The central thesis of the usable portion is that which fuel substrate you burn during exercise (fat vs. carbs) is irrelevant both for muscular adaptations and for how you should fuel on the bike. The body is an "excellent energy accountant," particularly sensitive to energy deficits, and does not simply replace glycogen in a one-to-one fashion based on what was burned. Carbs are the practical choice for on-bike fueling regardless of exercise intensity because they are easy to absorb, digest, affordable, and shelf-stable. The episode was intended to cover additional myths about metabolism, fat oxidation, fueling strategies, metabolic adaptation, and low energy availability (LEA), but this content is lost to corruption.

Key Training Recommendations

  • Fuel rides with carbohydrates regardless of exercise intensity — do not try to match fuel intake to substrate burned (Kolie)
  • Do not restrict on-bike carb intake because you are "mostly burning fat" at Zone 2; the body needs energy as energy, not substrate-matched replacement (Kolie)
  • Use kilojoules from your power meter as a rough 1:1 estimate of kilocalories burned (assumes ~20% human efficiency), but recognize this is an approximation with meaningful error bars (Kolie)
  • Do not obsess over calorie-counting precision to the level of 10-50 calories; food labels themselves carry significant measurement uncertainty (Kolie & Kyle)
  • Consult a qualified sports nutritionist or dietitian (preferably cycling-specific) for personalized fueling or weight-loss guidance rather than relying solely on podcast advice (Kolie)

Caveats & Qualifications

  • Kolie explicitly states he is not a nutritionist or dietitian; this is coaching experience plus his reading of the literature over the prior ~6 months
  • The fuel substrate discussion references their earlier Watts Doc metabolism series (10-15 episodes) for deeper evidence; this episode is a summary/different angle, not the primary source
  • Calorie and substrate calculations from power meters are approximations — the body's energy accounting is not precise to small calorie differences either
  • Sensitivity warning issued: topics include body weight, energy deficits, metabolic adaptation, and LEA, which may be triggering for some listeners

Contrarian or Non-Obvious Claims

  • Which fuel substrate you burn during exercise is categorically irrelevant to the muscular adaptations you get from training (Kolie, referencing their Watts Doc series conclusion)
  • The body is much more precise at tracking energy deficits than energy surpluses — evolutionary asymmetry favoring fat storage means surplus is "welcomed" while deficit triggers strong compensatory responses (Kolie)
  • Trying to eat fats on the bike to "match" fat burning during Zone 2 is not only unnecessary but impractical — carbs are superior on-bike fuel for practical reasons alone (Kolie & Kyle)
