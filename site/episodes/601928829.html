3. Threshold, FTP & Performance Testing

Core Message

The 2×20-minute workout is a solid default FTP session, but its value comes from the metabolic time spent at threshold intensity rather than any magic in the "20 minutes" number. The first 2–3 minutes of any interval are spent with heart rate and oxygen delivery lagging behind power output, meaning a 20-minute interval yields only ~15–17 minutes of true metabolic FTP work. This creates a minimum effective interval duration of about 10 minutes. The bigger principle is that continuous FTP work is superior to fragmented FTP work, and progressive overload must come primarily from increasing total time in zone rather than increasing power.

Key Training Recommendations

  • Minimum effective duration for an FTP interval is approximately 10 minutes (which yields ~7 minutes of true metabolic FTP work); intervals shorter than this provide diminishing returns.
  • Total metabolic time at FTP intensity per session should be at least 30 minutes. 2×20 (yielding ~33–35 min true metabolic work) meets this threshold.
  • Progress by adding time, not power: go from 2×20 → 3×15 → 3×20 → 4×15 → 4×20, etc. Resist the temptation to increase watts.
  • Continuous intervals are better than fragmented ones: 1×40 > 2×20 > 4×10, even at slightly lower power. Example: 1×40 min at 230–235 W is approximately as effective as 2×20 at 245 W (given 250 W FTP).
  • Assign FTP intervals at ~95–97%, not 100%, to reduce fatigue and allow consistent quality across all intervals in the session.
  • Pace the first interval conservatively — going 10–15 W over FTP on interval 1 will leave you 10–15 W under FTP on interval 2, defeating the purpose of the workout.
  • For time-crunched athletes (40–60 min available): prefer fewer, longer continuous intervals over many short ones. With only 30 min of work time after warm-up, a single 30-minute effort at 90–95% FTP is optimal.
  • For high-volume athletes (2–3 hr rides): embed FTP intervals inside longer rides (e.g., ride 1 hr easy, 2×20 in the middle, 1 hr easy). As TTE extends, scale to 3×30 or even 1×80+ for very high responders.
  • Recovery between FTP intervals: keep legs moving at a pace just enough to preserve warmup benefit; for high-volume long rides, can raise recovery to 50–70% FTP to add training stress.
  • Do not increase power during a training block; increase time and retest FTP at block end.

Caveats & Qualifications

  • The progression to very long single FTP intervals (1×60, 1×80+) is only appropriate for highly experienced athletes with many hours per week. Beginners attempting this will have a "really bad day."
  • Training styles of elite athletes do not necessarily transfer to intermediate or beginner athletes.
  • Going over FTP (even just 3–5%) causes blood lactate to rise, accelerating glycogen depletion. This is not a better training stimulus — it just depletes you faster and curtails total time at the right metabolic intensity.

Contrarian or Non-Obvious Claims

  • Riding 10 Watts over FTP for an FTP interval is no better than riding at FTP, and likely worse, because it reduces total completable time at the target metabolic state.
  • The 2×20 structure is good largely because it's the minimum dose that provides enough true metabolic time at FTP. It's not that 20 minutes has physiological magic — it's that you can't go much shorter and still get adequate stimulus.
  • Training at 95% FTP produces essentially the same metabolic stimulus as 100% FTP, but with lower fatigue — meaning more total training work can be accumulated.
