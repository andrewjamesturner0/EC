Ten Minute Tips 8 Back Squat Stance

Ten Minute Tips 1,737 words View transcript

Referenced in

7. Strength Training

Core Message

Kyle Helson covers bar position (high bar vs. low bar) and foot placement for the barbell back squat. The key message is that both are spectrums, not binaries — athletes should experiment to find what's comfortable for their individual anatomy rather than rigidly adhering to one position. EMG studies show that glute and quad activation is essentially the same across high bar, low bar, and front squat positions, so for cyclists (who aren't competitive lifters), the exact bar position doesn't matter.

Key Training Recommendations

  • Do not use a barbell pad — it covers the knurling and makes the bar more likely to slip at heavier weights
  • Squeeze traps and pull shoulder blades back to create a "shelf" for the bar to sit on
  • If you have limited ankle/knee mobility, a lower bar position will likely be easier (less forward knee travel required)
  • Experiment with stance width using socks on a smooth floor: squat down and wiggle feet to find what feels most comfortable and allows the deepest range of motion
  • Toe angle, stance width, and bar position should all be individualized based on hip socket and femur morphology
  • Don't obsess over adding 5 more pounds to your squat — cycling is your sport, not powerlifting

Caveats & Qualifications

  • Hip socket and femur anatomy set ultimate limits on squat stance — mobility work helps but won't overcome structural constraints
  • The "low bar = better posterior chain" claim is not supported by EMG research; both positions produce similar glute and quad activation

Contrarian or Non-Obvious Claims

  • High bar vs. low bar squat distinction doesn't matter for cyclists — EMG data shows equivalent muscle activation across positions including front squats
  • The specific squat variation matters far less than people think; some athletes may need to use split squats, front squats, or leg press instead of back squats, and that's completely fine
