Perspectives the Challenges of Self Coaching with Alex Carmona

Perspectives 7,639 words View transcript

Referenced in

12. Racing & Team Tactics

Core Message

Alex Carmona (Empirical Cycling coach) discusses his journey from coached athlete to self-coached athlete to coach. The central themes are self-coaching challenges (accountability, objectivity, flexibility vs. structure), race intelligence as a separate skill from fitness, and the importance of mentors. Alex argues that knowing your own strengths and weaknesses — both physiological and tactical — is the fundamental prerequisite for racing improvement, beyond simple fitness gains.

Key Training Recommendations

  • Study courses and opponents before races: "Do the extra leg work. If you want to win a race, you know it's only these rare freaks that just show up and ride away and win." Course knowledge = tactical advantage
  • Map your winning scenario before a race and work backwards: "What's your winning scenario, then let's work backwards. So it really does require a good analysis of somebody's skill set and also their mindset"
  • If you have a modest sprint (~600 W), you cannot get separation in a men's crit field — you must follow an attack or "coax somebody, give them a gel to attack" to get out
  • Self-coaching requires writing out a periodized plan (events, goals, timeline) in advance; without the structure of a pre-set plan, flexibility degrades into unstructured riding
  • When structuring self-coaching, treat intervals like a program by RPE — "not only intervals by RPE, but a whole program by RPE almost" — adjusting based on daily feel rather than rigid watts
  • Get to the front of a field before key terrain features: "There's gonna be a climb here, well then you got to make sure you're at the front of the group going to that climb"

Caveats & Qualifications

  • Self-coaching works best if you have strong self-discipline and have been coached for long enough to internalize training structure; it can easily drift toward Strava comparison and unstructured riding
  • Downside of self-coaching: missing exposure to methods you wouldn't think of yourself; "if you're getting coached by someone else, you're going to be doing stuff that you didn't think of"
  • Race results alone don't tell you what to fix — you have to "pay attention to those outcomes and think about what they mean"

Contrarian or Non-Obvious Claims

  • "People often say there'll be like a lull 15, 20 minutes into a race [crit]. It just didn't." High-level crits are full gas from the gun — the assumption of a neutral period is wrong at the national level.
  • Tactical intelligence is a trainable skill: watching YouTube videos of crits to understand sprint setups and breakaway coverage is legitimate preparation, separate from fitness work

Evolving Views

  • Alex evolved from thinking that showing up to races every weekend and riding hard would produce improvement, to realizing that at Cat 2+ you need to "understand the races better, understand the courses better, understand who you're racing against better" — fitness alone plateaus; race craft must be actively developed.
